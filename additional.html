ALUMNI, WELCOME BACK!

Tallinn University of Technology believes that its success is measured by the success of its alumni! We keep the alumni community engaged through close collaboration, development opportunities, and shared values. The TalTech Alumni Center ensures that alumni have the opportunity to ATTEND the university events and programs, ADVANCE through different education opportunities and courses, COMMUNICATE with each other and the university through a strong network and cooperation, and CONTRIBUTE to the university's development in various ways. Let's stay connected and support the growth of our shared alma mater!

ATTEND: FIND YOUR FAVOURITE EVENTS

The university and the Alumni Association organize various events throughout the year! All alumni, employees, students and partners are pleased to join with their companions. Sports events, annual ball, alumni gatherings - we have it all!

ADVANCE: CONTINUE YOUR EDUCATION

Earn a higher education degree flexibly or enhance your knowledge alongside work. With a wide range of study formats and topics, you can choose from nearly 50 exciting microdegrees or take continuing education courses to boost your career!

CONTRIBUTE: SUPPORT THE UNIVERSITY AND THE TALENTS

The goal of the Tallinn University of Technology Development Fund is to support talented young students, researchers, and lecturers. Scholarships provided by the fund's partners enable students to focus on their studies and recognize employees whose dedication serves as an inspiration to others.

COMMUNICATE: CONTACT THE UNIVERSITY AND FELLOW ALUMNI

Stay connected with the university and your fellow alumni through our strong network and collaborative opportunities. Join events, engage in discussions, and share your experiences to strengthen our community. Your connections today can create new opportunities for tomorrow!

TalTech Student Organisations

TalTech offers a wide variety of student organisations where you can develop skills, meet new friends, and enrich your university life.From robotics and entrepreneurship to arts, sports, and international exchange, there’s something for everyone!

BEST-Estonia

Part of the Board of European Students of Technology, BEST connects TalTech students with peers across 32 countries, offering training, travel, and international collaboration.

ESN TalTech International Club

A local section of Erasmus Student Network, ESN TalTech IC organizes cultural events, trips, and language cafés to support international and local students.

Formula Student Team Tallinn

Students design, build, and compete with electric formula cars in international competitions, gaining hands-on engineering and business experience.

Solaride

An ambitious project to build a solar car and compete globally, while training future engineers and innovators through the Solaride Academy.

Robotics Club

With over 250 enthusiasts, the Robotics Club develops drones, autonomous systems, and wall-climbing robots, while competing in Baltic and international contests.

Startup Garage

A hub for entrepreneurial students to network, develop business ideas, and participate in events like the Youth Startup Summit.

Current Research and Innovation Projects

The Ragnar Nurkse Department of Innovation and Governance is an internationally recognized interdisciplinary research center. It focuses on socially relevant and future-oriented issues such as e-governance, smart cities, digital transformation, fiscal governance, and sustainability transitions.

CITYAM

Funded by Interreg BSR (2023–2025), CITYAM explores Urban Air Mobility and the role of drones in cities. The project develops strategies and tools for sustainable airspace management and city planning.

RADAR

Horizon Europe project (2025–2029) focusing on democratic governance of public services. It aims to strengthen legitimacy through democratic anchorage, new narratives, and education.

Green Transition Possibilities

Funded by the Estonian Ministry of Economic Affairs (2025–2026), this project analyzes opportunities for Estonia’s sustainable economic development and designs new policy interventions for the 2027–2034 budget period.

LIFE-COMET

LIFE Programme project (2023–2026) creating community energy coalitions in Eastern EU. It provides technical, legal, and advocacy support to stimulate citizen participation in energy initiatives.

ROBUST

H2020 project (2022–2026) on crisis governance. It shifts focus from resilience (“bouncing back”) to robustness (“building back better”), delivering new strategies for democratic institutions in turbulent times.

UrbanSplash

Estonian Ministry of Education project (2024–2027) developing real-time monitoring of bathing water quality using IoT sensors, meteorological, and geospatial data. Piloted in Tartu and Dublin.
