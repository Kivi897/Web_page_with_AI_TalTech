Tallinn University of Technology

Established in 1918, TalTech is the only technical university in Estonia. Located in Tallinn, we offer world-class education in engineering, business, public administration, and maritime affairs. With over 11,000 students, including 1,500 international students from around the world, TalTech prepares graduates for successful careers with our motto "Mente et manu" - With wisdom and hands.

TalTech History and Milestones

Founded on September 17, 1918, as the Estonian Engineering Society's Special Engineering Courses, TalTech emerged from Estonia's urgent need for locally trained engineering specialists. In the early 20th century, young Estonians had to travel to St. Petersburg, Germany, or Riga for engineering education. TalTech was established to provide Estonian-based education adapted to local conditions and needs during Estonia's independence movement. The university has evolved through significant historical periods, from its founding during Estonia's first independence to maintaining operations through challenging times, and now stands as Estonia's premier technical university with international recognition and partnerships.

1918
Estonian Engineering Society opens Special Engineering Courses - the foundation of TalTech. Programs offered in mechanical, electrical, civil and hydraulic engineering, shipbuilding, and architecture.
1919
School becomes private Tallinn College of Engineering
1920
Tallinn College of Engineering declared a state institution
1923
First engineering graduation theses defended in Estonia; state laboratory of materials testing opens. Teachers' efforts to develop Estonian terminology for science and technology proved fruitful, with first engineering books published.
1936
Act of the Head of State establishes Tallinn Technical Institute
1940s-1990s
TalTech continued operations through challenging periods, maintaining engineering education and research despite political changes
1991
Estonia regained independence; TalTech began modernization and internationalization efforts
2000s
TalTech expanded programs in business, public administration, and maritime affairs; established international partnerships with BALTECH, NORDTEK, UNICA, SEFI, EUA, EAIE, and NAFSA
Today
TalTech serves over 11,000 students (including 1,500 international) with 985 academic staff (168 international), maintaining its position as Estonia's only technical university with a budget of €119 million
